Constitution of the Interstate Government Between the Contiguous United States of America

Article 1: The Territorial Claims of Interstate Governance

Section 1

The Interstate Constitution lays claim to all borders between states of the contiguous United States of America. The territorial extent of the Interstate Government is legally immutable and may only grow with the incorporation and establishment of adjacent states by the United States of America. The Interstate Constitution lays no claim to any territory located within state borders.

Section 2

Every unbroken border between two states forms a separate division of Interstate governance.

Section 3

An individual belongs to the interstate border to which they most closely reside. All individuals who belong to an interstate border may govern as prescribed by the Interstate Constitution. An indvidual who lives equidistant to the closest interstate borders do not belong to an Interstate division, but they may participate in actions sanctioned by an Interstate division department committee.
